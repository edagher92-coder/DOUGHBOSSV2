The DoughBoss ordering, kitchen-board, voucher and POS-integration platform is built and working — reviewed and hardened across multiple passes. What remains is mostly real business data, account setup and a handful of decisions, not further engineering. This brief is the working document for today's meeting: what's done, what it's cost, what's outstanding, and the questions to walk through with the team.

1Executive summary

The engine — online ordering across all three shops, Stripe card-payment plumbing, the live kitchen order board, the student voucher system, the POSPal till bridge, and a catering enquiry pipeline — is built and has been through several rounds of security and accessibility review. Revesby is connected, enabled and verified in POSPal (both $5/$10 coupon rules confirmed live); Bankstown and Roselands are not yet. The main remaining items are: a real end-to-end voucher test at Revesby, collecting the real menu and business details, confirming brand naming, and switching on the payment/notification layers that are already built but deliberately left off.

5Snow Boss — status & recommendation

What it is: Snow Boss is the sister dessert brand under the same hospitality group as Dough Boss (Revesby, Bankstown, Roselands) — confirmed in the draft licensing/wholesale agreement.

Already built

  • A dual-brand section on the demo site pairing Dough Boss + Snow Boss.
  • A working student-voucher prototype ($5/$10) that issues a real, redeemable code.

Not yet decided

  • Whether Snow Boss gets its own menu items inside the ordering system, or stays a voucher/cross-promotion tie-in.
  • Whether the drafted (unsigned) wholesale/licensing terms are meant to formalise Snow Boss as a licensable sub-brand, or it stays run in-house.

Recommendation: treat Snow Boss as a cross-promotion layer on the existing platform for now — the student voucher already does this well and is now launch-ready at Revesby (POSPal coupon-rule setup in §4 is done). Defer the full licensing/wholesale question and any separate Snow Boss menu build until the core Dough Boss rollout (menu, 3 shops, payments) is live and stable. Revisit Snow Boss as its own product line once Dough Boss is generating real order volume — launching both fully at once spreads the team thin on data entry, training and testing for no immediate payoff.

6Recommended rollout path

  1. Close out data collection this week — menu, shop details, ABN, brand naming (§4). This is the actual bottleneck, not further development.
  2. POSPal loop — done — Revesby's $5/$10 coupon rules are created and verified live; run one real end-to-end voucher test before trusting it for customers.
  3. Soft-launch Revesby only — it already has POSPal connected and can go live fastest. Use it to validate the kitchen board and voucher flow with real orders before rolling to the other two shops.
  4. Decide the payments path — switch Stripe on for the soft-launch, or run pickup/cash first and add cards once the flow is proven.
  5. Roll out shop #2 and #3 once Revesby is stable — mostly data entry + POSPal credentials, not new development.
  6. Snow Boss as phase two — launch the student voucher alongside the Dough Boss rollout (it's nearly ready), hold deeper Snow Boss build until after all 3 shops are live.
  7. Switch on the "nice to have" layers last — SMS, staff push and receipt printing are built and tested but off; turn them on one at a time after go-live so any issues are easy to isolate.
