Straight answers

The questions you’re right to ask

“It’s a lot to pay each week.”
At $800/week plus 5% (capped at 15%), you typically pay well under what Uber Eats and Menulog take at 7–35%. Past a modest order volume, the system pays for itself — and the cap means it can never reach app rates.
“Will my staff actually use it?”
The kitchen sees one clear iPad board — simpler than juggling app tablets. We train your team on-site and stay on hand through the first busy weeks.
“Who handles the hosting and tech?”
It runs on your existing WordPress site with standard, proven tools. We set it up, maintain it, and you’ve got a friend on the phone — not a call centre.
“Switching off the apps sounds risky.”
You don’t switch anything off on day one. Run this alongside the apps, watch the direct orders grow, then wind the apps back on your terms.
“Is it really mine, or am I renting?”
It’s yours — your domain, your Stripe account, your customer data, your menu. The weekly fee is for running and supporting it, not for permission to use your own shop.
“What does it cost to start?”
Setup is roughly $4,000–5,000 at cost to build and launch across the three shops. Then $800/week + 5% (capped 15%) to run, maintain and market it — Stripe’s ~1.7% + 30c per card is separate and goes to Stripe.
