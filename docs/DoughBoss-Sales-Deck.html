Section 01 · Why change anything

The apps rent you your own customers

Uber Eats and Menulog bring orders — then take a cut of every one, keep the customer’s details, and bury your brand inside theirs. The busier you get, the more it costs.

7–35%
skimmed off every order
Commission on each sale — it scales up exactly as your volume grows, with no ceiling.
0%
of the customer is yours
Their name, number and order history live in the app. You can’t market to them, ever.
~$3,000
gone in a single busy week
On ~$12k of weekly online sales at ~25%, that’s roughly $150k a year leaving the business.

You’re building someone else’s asset. Every order grows their customer list — not yours.

Section 02 · The fix

It all runs in the phone’s browser. Nothing for the customer to install, nothing for staff to juggle.

Section 07 · The questions you’re right to ask

Straight answers

“It’s a lot to pay each week.”
At $800/week plus 5% (capped at 15%), you typically pay well under what Uber Eats and Menulog take at 7–35%. Past a modest volume, the system pays for itself — and the cap means it can never reach app rates.
“Will my staff actually use it?”
The kitchen sees one clear iPad board — simpler than juggling app tablets. We train your team on-site and stay on hand through the first busy weeks.
“Who handles the hosting and tech?”
It runs on your existing WordPress site with standard, proven tools. We set it up, maintain it, and you’ve got a friend on the phone — not a call centre.
“Switching off the apps sounds risky.”
You don’t switch anything off on day one. Run this alongside the apps, watch the direct orders grow, then wind the apps back on your terms.
“Is it really mine, or am I renting?”
It’s yours — your domain, your Stripe account, your customer data, your menu. The weekly fee is for running and supporting it, not for permission to use your own shop.
“What if it breaks on a Friday night?”
It’s built to keep taking orders even if a screen drops, and you can reach us directly. We watch the first busy weekends closely so issues are caught early.
