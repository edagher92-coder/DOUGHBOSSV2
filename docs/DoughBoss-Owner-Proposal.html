1. The big idea — own your orders

Right now, the big delivery apps take a large slice of every sale, and they keep your customers as their customers. With your own ordering system, that changes completely. You pay zero commission on every order — the money from a A$30 pizza stays with you, not split with Uber Eats or Menulog. Just as importantly, the customers stay yours: their names, phone numbers and favourite orders. That means you can bring them back again and again, on your terms, without paying a middleman every time.

2. Where you are today

Here's the good news: the ordering system is already built and sitting on your website. The not-yet-done part is simply that it was never switched on. Today there are no menu items entered, the order pages aren't published, and the prices are set in US dollars with no GST. There's also no live way for the kitchen to see orders, and no online card payment yet. None of this is a problem — it's the final setup, like a new oven that's installed but not yet plugged in.

3. How it works

  1. A customer opens your website, browses the menu, and builds their pizza.
  2. They check out — paying by card online, or choosing pay-on-pickup.
  3. The kitchen screen lights up with an alarm the instant the order lands. Nobody has to watch a phone.
  4. Staff tap once to Accept (and give a time), then Preparing, then Ready — three taps.
  5. The customer watches the live status on their phone, so they know exactly when to collect.

5. Online payments — in simple terms

Customers pay by card in Australian dollars, with GST handled correctly so your books stay clean. Apple Pay and Google Pay are included, so people pay in a couple of taps. There's no monthly lock-in for card payments — you pay a small fee per order, only when money actually comes in (about 1.7% + 30c an order, far below app commissions). Prefer cash at the counter? You can also offer pay-on-pickup.

6. Built to grow

The system handles a busy Friday night without breaking a sweat — whether ten orders or a hundred land in an hour, each one lights up the kitchen screen in turn, in the order it arrived. Adding a second or third shop later is straightforward: each new location gets its own menu, hours and order board.

And here's the best part — every new shop is commission-free too. You never start paying a slice per order, no matter how many shops you open.

11. Extra upside (beyond commission)

You own 100% of the customer list — apps never hand that over. Conservative, illustrative examples:

  • Repeat orders: a 5% lift in repeat purchases on a $3,200/week base ≈ +$690/month, won via cheap text/email instead of paid app placement.
  • Fewer missed/wrong orders: digital tickets cut errors — even 2 saved orders/week at $32 ≈ $280/month in food & refunds avoided.
  • Faster kitchen flow: the live board sequences orders, freeing staff during the rush.

12. Marketing that gets cheaper over time

Because you own your customer list, you can advertise straight to people who already love your pizza — a fraction of the cost of chasing strangers. We start with Facebook and Instagram, where your local fans already are, then move into TikTok as you grow. We'll also help you show up on Google when people search “pizza near me,” so new customers find you for free. Over time, your own audience does more of the work and your cost to win an order keeps dropping.
