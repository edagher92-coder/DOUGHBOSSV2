The opportunity

Why rebuild, why now

Make catering the engine — not an afterthought

Today the site sells single pizzas well. Catering is where the big, planned, high-margin orders live — and it's barely surfaced. The rebuild puts catering at the centre, keeps everyday ordering fast, and uses the pay-now layer we already built to turn enquiries into deposit-secured, board-routed bookings.

Higher-value orders

Catering baskets are $150–$850 vs a ~$36 walk-in order. One booking ≈ 5–20 normal orders, planned in advance.

Own the relationship

Commission-free, on your domain, with leads captured — recurring office & club accounts become a sales pipeline you own.

Pay-now ready

The Stripe layer (2.5.0) already verifies payments server-side. Catering reuses it for deposits + balance — minimal new build.

The brief: rebuild functionality, GUI, FE/BE integration and APIs — with catering and pay-now at the core.

Architecture & integration

Reuse what we built · add only what's new

How it's wired

Front-end

Hybrid FSE block theme + vanilla-JS islands. Owner edits catering/marketing pages in WordPress; the ordering/builder/Stripe/KDS stay as shortcode islands (no rewrite). No bundler. CWV budget, self-host Bebas, conditional asset loading, AVIF/WebP images.

Back-end

New catering package CPT (owner-editable) + a new enquiries table with a lifecycle (new → quoted → deposit_paid → confirmed → balance → fulfilled). Reuse orders/locations/KDS routing. New admin "Catering" screen; DB version bump + migration.

Payments & APIs

Extend pay-now: two PaymentIntents (deposit now, balance near event) + saved Customer; webhook (payment_intent.succeeded, refunds) as source of truth; refunds UI; GST tax invoices. Analytics from existing hooks; SEO schema (LocalBusiness/Menu/Offer/FAQ/Review).
