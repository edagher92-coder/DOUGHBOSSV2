PART A — BRAND & SYSTEM LICENCE AGREEMENT

How to use this template (Part A). Complete every [PLACEHOLDER], choose between the marked alternatives (e.g. exclusive vs non-exclusive in clause 3, GST-inclusive vs exclusive in the fees), delete all italic guidance notes, and have an NSW solicitor review it before issuing it to a prospective licensee. Pair it with a completed Schedule 1 (commercial terms). If you are also licensing the DoughBoss/POSPal software, complete clause 9 and Schedule 4.

Plain-English summary (Part A). This agreement lets another business owner operate as a "Dough Boss" outlet — using your name, recipes, menu, look and operating system — in a set area, for a set time, in exchange for an upfront fee plus an ongoing royalty and a marketing contribution. You keep ownership of the brand, recipes and software (they are only licensed, never sold), and the operator must meet your quality, halal and food-safety standards. If they break the rules or stop paying, you can end the licence and they must stop using the brand and hand back your materials.
